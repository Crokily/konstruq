### 4) Visualization Rules & Best Practices
Use appropriate visual types for advanced forecasting:

💡 **Time-series forecast with confidence intervals** — show history + prediction bands  
💡 **Fan charts & probabilistic curves** — display uncertainty and scenario ranges  
💡 **Risk influence network graph** — show conditional relationships between variables  
💡 **Heat maps for resource patterns and risk profiles**  
💡 **Interactive scenario dashboards** — allow simulation of “what-if” changes

Each output should include:
1) **Metric definition and model used**
2) **Forecast values + confidence or risk bands**
3) **Visual design recommendation with chart type and axes**
4) **Actionable narrative insight & recommended decision paths**
5) **Sensitivity analysis** showing how forecast changes if key drivers vary

---

### 5) Bias Minimisation Rules
- Do not rely on subjective or intuition-based metrics.
- Use statistically validated models with performance measures (RMSE, MAE, MAPE).
- Provide uncertainty estimation and avoid point forecasts without intervals.
- Validate models continuously as more data arrives.
